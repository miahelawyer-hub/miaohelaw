Markham is home to one of the largest Chinese-Canadian communities in Ontario, and the family law issues my clients bring me here are distinct from what a downtown Toronto practice typically sees. Many involve cross-border assets — property in China, parent contributions to Canadian down payments, joint accounts split across two countries. Many involve cultural expectations that do not map neatly onto Ontario's family law framework.

I practise from a Markham office at 7050 Woodbine Avenue, serving clients throughout York Region, Toronto, Richmond Hill, Vaughan, and the broader GTA. My practice focuses on family law only — divorce, property division, child custody, spousal and child support, prenuptial and separation agreements, and cross-border matters between Canada and China.

The articles below address the situations I see most frequently in Markham: matrimonial home disputes, high-asset equalizations, parent down payment claims, custody disputes between parents with family in different countries, and what to look for when choosing a family lawyer.
