This hub collects my family-law writing in one place. Ontario family disputes are rarely “one-issue” cases — property, parenting, and support interact, and the best decisions are usually the ones made early: defining the separation timeline, organizing evidence, building a disclosure package, and choosing a path that matches your risk tolerance and budget.

The articles below are written to be practical. They focus on what clients actually need to decide: what facts matter most, what documents move a file forward, how courts tend to analyze recurring problems (equalization, the matrimonial home, support), and how cross-border realities between Canada and China affect evidence and enforceability.

This hub is particularly relevant to bilingual families and clients with cross-border ties between Canada and China — disputes where Mandarin-language documentation, WeChat evidence, or assets located in mainland China interact with Ontario court procedures.
