## What Makes Newmarket Court Different

Newmarket operates as a **Unified Family Court (UFC)**. Every judge assigned to divorce and family law cases there deals exclusively with family law — no criminal cases, no civil litigation. Just family law, day in and day out.

For clients, this is actually good news. Judges who specialize in family law develop consistent, predictable approaches to issues like [property division](/en/blog/ontario-divorce-property-division/), child custody, and spousal support. An experienced lawyer who appears regularly at Newmarket Court can anticipate how judges are likely to approach your specific situation — and build your strategy around that.

---

## The Challenges of Newmarket Court

That said, Newmarket Court comes with its own set of procedural demands.

**High volume, longer timelines.** Newmarket handles an enormous caseload. Without careful planning, delays compound quickly. A lawyer who doesn't manage the timeline proactively will cost you months.

**The DRO Conference.** Newmarket Court strongly encourages parties to attend a **Dispute Resolution Officer (DRO) Conference** before proceeding to a Case Conference. This is arranged directly between counsel — it's not something the court schedules for you. Many lawyers unfamiliar with Newmarket's local practice miss this step entirely, which disrupts the natural flow of the proceeding.

**Strict filing deadlines.** Newmarket enforces document submission deadlines firmly. Miss a deadline, and your scheduled Conference may not proceed. That's not just an inconvenience — it can shift your negotiating position and push resolution further away.

## Knowing When to Bring a Motion — and What Kind

At Newmarket Court, most motions are scheduled as **Regular Motions (one hour)**. But not every situation calls for a Regular Motion. Sometimes you need an **Urgent Motion**. Sometimes you need to request leave from the court for a **Long Motion**.

Getting this wrong doesn't just waste a court date. It can mean losing the opportunity to protect a client's interests at a critical moment.
